Great Expectations

Chapter IV: Christmas Day and the Missing Pie

The soldiers, after Joe mended their handcuffs, departed. We proceeded to the annual Christmas dinner, where the usual guests were assembled: Mr. Wopsle, the parish clerk; Uncle Pumblechook, a well-to-do corn-chandler; and Mr. and Mrs. Hubble.
The whole dinner was a prolonged torment for me. The guests constantly lectured me on the wickedness of youth and the duty of gratitude to my sister, which only amplified my guilt over the theft.
Uncle Pumblechook, in particular, repeatedly stressed how indebted I was to my sister for raising me "by hand." Every comment felt like a direct accusation.
As the conversation became more severe, I thought of running away from the table, but the stern eye of my sister prevented me. I tried to relieve my distress by drinking a large quantity of the "savory" brandy I had replaced with tar-water.
Finally, the centerpiece of the dinner—the pork-pie—was brought out. Mrs. Joe announced proudly how she had protected it, and how she hoped I was grateful for such a rare treat.
I knew the pie was gone. My guilt reached a breaking point. I started crying and was about to confess everything when Mrs. Joe, turning to the table, declared, "Now, here's the pie!"
She then discovered the pie was missing. Before the alarm could truly erupt, there came a tremendous **BANG** at the door, and the whole company started. We all cried out, "The police!"
