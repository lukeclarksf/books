Great Expectations

Part I: Childhood and the Discovery of Hope (Chapters 1–19)

Ch. Summary Key Events / Characters
I Pip, a young orphan, encounters an escaped convict in the marsh churchyard and is forced to help him steal food and a file. First meeting with the Convict (Magwitch). Introduction to Pip, Joe, and Mrs. Joe.
II Pip steals the required items from his sister's house, feeling immense guilt. He nearly gets caught when his sister prepares for the Christmas dinner guests. Pip's theft of the "wittles" and the file. Introduction of Mr. Wopsle and Uncle Pumblechook.
III Pip delivers the supplies. He briefly encounters a second, wounded convict who flees. Soldiers arrive at Joe's house, asking him to mend shackles. The convicts are recaptured. The two convicts (Magwitch and Compeyson) are recaptured after a fight. Magwitch covers for Pip.
IV Mrs. Joe discovers the theft of the pork pie during Christmas dinner, but the moment is interrupted by the arrival of the soldiers. The near discovery of Pip's crime.
V The soldiers take Joe to mend the handcuffs. Pip observes the convicts' capture. The recapture and Magwitch's noble gesture.
VI Pip feels deeply burdened by his secret concerning the file and the food. He keeps the truth hidden from Joe, but his conscience is troubled. Pip's guilt and the start of his secrecy.
VII Pip is sent to Miss Havisham's house to "play." He meets Estella, who treats him with disdain and calls him "common." First visit to Satis House. Pip's instant shame and the birth of his social ambition.
VIII Pip returns from Satis House and lies about his experience, leading to Joe confiding in him. He decides to seek education to become less "common." Pip lies to Mrs. Joe and Pumblechook. Joe's kindness is highlighted.
IX Joe visits Miss Havisham to discuss Pip's future. Joe's awkward behavior in a formal setting further embarrasses Pip. Pip's increasing awareness of social class and Joe's "commonness."
X Miss Havisham pays Joe 25 guineas, confirming that Pip is to be officially apprenticed to the blacksmith trade. Pip's dreams are crushed. Pip starts his apprenticeship with Joe.
XI Pip encounters a mysterious man (later identified as Mr. Jaggers) at Satis House. Joe's journeyman, Dolge Orlick, argues with Mrs. Joe. Pip meets Jaggers. Growing antagonism between Mrs. Joe and Orlick.
XII Mrs. Joe is brutally attacked and suffers permanent brain damage. Pip suspects Orlick. Mrs. Joe's attack. Introduction of Orlick as a sinister figure.
XIII Pip spends a happy period helping Biddy care for Mrs. Joe and improve her education, finding comfort and satisfaction in the forge life. Biddy's influence. The family adapts to Mrs. Joe's injury.
XIV Pip encounters the strange man from Miss Havisham's house again, who gives him money. Pip recognizes him as the man with the large head and strong hands. The introduction of Jaggers's unique mannerisms.
XV Pip receives news that he has inherited a great fortune from an anonymous benefactor and must leave immediately for London to become a gentleman. **The Turning Point:** The Great Expectation is announced.
XVI Pip says emotional goodbyes to Joe and Biddy, feeling shame over their simple ways but excited about his new life. Pip’s snobbery begins.
XVII Pip visits Miss Havisham, believing she is his benefactor. She encourages his belief and tells him Estella will soon return from school. Miss Havisham reinforces Pip's misconception.
XVIII Pip receives his new clothes and prepares for his departure, treating the townspeople, especially Pumblechook, with contempt. Pip’s final days in the village.
XIX Pip leaves his home and begins his journey to London, reflecting on the changes and hopes for his future. The transition from Pip's childhood to his adult life.

Part II: The Gentleman's Life and Revelation (Chapters 20–39)

Ch. Summary Key Events / Characters
XX Pip arrives in London and meets Mr. Jaggers, his guardian. He sees Jaggers's office and observes his cold, intimidating personality. Pip begins his life in London.
XXI Pip meets his roommate, **Herbert Pocket**, at Barnard's Inn. Herbert is the pale young gentleman Pip fought years ago. Pip moves into Barnard's Inn and gains a kind friend.
XXII Herbert reveals Miss Havisham's tragic history: she was betrayed by her fiancé (Compeyson) on her wedding day. He nicknames Pip "Handel." Pip learns Miss Havisham's backstory.
XXIII Pip meets Matthew Pocket, Herbert’s father and his tutor, and the other strange students. Introduction of the Pocket family at Hammersmith.
XXIV Pip attempts to describe Jaggers's unusual house to Herbert. Details of Jaggers's intimidating home life.
XXV Pip visits Wemmick at Walworth and meets the Aged Parent. Introduction of Wemmick's dual life (private/public).
XXVI Pip attends a dinner at Jaggers's house and observes the lawyer's control over his housekeeper, Molly. Molly's strange history is hinted at.
XXVII Pip is surprised by a visit from Joe, whom he treats poorly out of social embarrassment. Joe delivers a message from Estella. Pip's snobbery reaches its peak as he shames Joe.
XXVIII Pip travels back to his hometown. He encounters the convict from the pub who gave him money many years ago. Pip's return trip. He learns Orlick is now a gate-keeper at Satis House.
XXIX Pip sees Estella again, and she informs him she has no heart and cannot love him, yet Pip remains obsessed. Pip and Estella meet again. She warns him of her coldness.
XXX Pip and Herbert visit the theatre where Mr. Wopsle is performing. Pip dislikes the performance and finds the company common. Pip's continued snobbery.
XXXI Pip tries to prevent Orlick from working at Satis House, believing he is dangerous. Pip gets Orlick fired.
XXXII Estella arrives in London, and Pip escorts her. Estella begins her life in London high society.
XXXIII Estella continues to caution Pip that she is incapable of love, even as she uses men to fuel her power. Estella's psychological cruelty is evident.
XXXIV Pip and Herbert rack up debts as they live extravagant and wasteful lives. The financial recklessness of Pip's "Expectation."
XXXV Mrs. Joe dies. Pip returns home for the funeral, reflecting on his shame and his poor treatment of Joe and Biddy. Mrs. Joe's death. Pip's remorse.
XXXVI Pip turns 21 and officially gains control of his money through Jaggers, but his benefactor remains anonymous. Pip reaches his majority.
XXXVII Pip uses his newfound money to secretly establish Herbert in business, arranging for him to be an anonymous partner. Pip performs a truly unselfish act.
XXXVIII Estella marries Bentley Drummle, a wealthy but brutish suitor, shocking and devastating Pip. Estella's marriage. Miss Havisham gloats in Estella's ability to inflict pain.
XXXIX On a stormy night, Pip's anonymous benefactor reveals himself: it is **Abel Magwitch**, the convict Pip helped in the churchyard. **The Great Discovery.** Pip's dreams are shattered as he learns the true, criminal source of his wealth.

Part III: Disaster and Redemption (Chapters 40–59)

Ch. Summary Key Events / Characters
XL Magwitch recounts his life story to Pip, including his association with the master criminal, **Compeyson**. Pip decides he cannot accept Magwitch's money. Magwitch's history is revealed.
XLI Pip shares the secret with Herbert. They realize Magwitch is in danger from Compeyson and the police, and they devise a plan to smuggle him out of the country. Pip and Herbert begin their loyal mission to save Magwitch.
XLII Magwitch reveals that Compeyson was Miss Havisham's cruel fiancé. The two major plotlines (the convict and Miss Havisham) are finally connected.
XLIII Pip confronts Bentley Drummle, who boasts about his control over Estella. Pip's final painful encounter with Drummle.
XLIV Pip realizes he has mistaken Miss Havisham for his benefactor and confronts her. He begs her to help Herbert financially. Pip's disillusionment is complete.
XLV Miss Havisham, remorseful, agrees to give Herbert the money. Pip sees a vision of her burning. Miss Havisham's guilt and Pip's strange premonition.
XLVI Pip returns to Satis House and finds Miss Havisham badly burned, having accidentally set herself on fire. Pip saves her, burning himself severely. Miss Havisham's tragic end. Pip shows heroic loyalty.
XLVII Jaggers's housekeeper, Molly, is identified as Estella's mother and Magwitch is her father. **The Final Revelation**: Jaggers's secrets are exposed.
XLVIII Pip and Herbert plot the escape plan. Pip sees Orlick lurking nearby. The danger to Magwitch increases.
XLIX Pip learns that Magwitch's daughter, whom he believed dead, is Estella. The climax of the mystery is revealed.
L Pip receives a mysterious note to meet someone. The setup for a confrontation.
LI Pip is lured to the sluice-house by a message from Orlick, who attempts to murder him. Pip is saved by Herbert and Startop. Orlick's true nature is confirmed, and he is captured.
LII Pip and Herbert attempt to row Magwitch to a steamer ship. They are interrupted by a pursuing boat containing Compeyson and the police. The attempted escape is foiled.
LIII Magwitch and Compeyson struggle in the water. Compeyson drowns, and Magwitch is captured, badly injured. Magwitch is captured, but Compeyson is dead.
LIV Magwitch is tried and sentenced to death. Pip stays by his side, showing immense loyalty and love. Magwitch's final, honorable trial.
LV Magwitch, badly injured and sentenced to death, dies peacefully after Pip tells him that his daughter, Estella, is alive. Pip falls gravely ill. Magwitch's death. Pip's emotional transformation is complete.
LVI Pip is arrested for debt. Joe comes to London, nurses Pip back to health, and pays off his debts. Joe's ultimate goodness and Pip's final humbling.
LVII Once Pip is recovered, Joe quietly returns home, leaving Pip a note that signifies their friendship is restored. Restoration of Pip and Joe's relationship.
LVIII Pip returns to the forge to ask Biddy to marry him, only to find that she and Joe have married each other. Pip gives them his blessing and leaves for Cairo to work with Herbert. Joe and Biddy's wedding. Pip accepts his new destiny.
LIX **The Final Chapter (Revised Ending):** Pip returns to Satis House years later and finds Estella, now a widow, walking in the garden. They part, implying they will not do so again. Pip and Estella meet again, suggesting a future based on shared sorrow and wisdom.
